## English summary

Canonical international reference on mango by Morton (1987), hosted by Purdue NewCROP. Covers botany, climate, soil, propagation, pests, diseases, and harvest. Used as the universal-principle anchor in the Kaset Atlas mango profile, with Thailand applicability flagged where local context differs.

## Notable citable facts

- Mango thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with a clear dry period before flowering.
- Full sun is required for vigorous growth and fruit development.
- Major pests: leafhoppers, fruit flies, scale, mealybugs, seed weevil.
- Major diseases: anthracnose (Colletotrichum gloeosporioides), powdery mildew.

## Notes

Older publication (1987). Cultural and pest data have aged differently — prefer Thai sources for cultivar lists and Thai-specific pests; defer to Morton for general botany and global pest taxonomy.
